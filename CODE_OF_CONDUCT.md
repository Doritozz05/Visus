# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment
As maintainers and contributors of Visus, we are dedicated to building a welcoming and inclusive community. We pledge to treat everyone with respect, regardless of their background, identity, or level of technical expertise. Our goal is to ensure that participating in this project is a safe and productive experience for every individual.

## Professional Standards
We foster a community where:
*   Communication is professional, constructive, and kind.
*   Diverse perspectives and experiences are valued.
*   Discussions are focused on the project's goals and technical merit.
*   Critique is aimed at ideas and code, never at individuals.

## Unacceptable Behavior
We do not tolerate harassment or unprofessional conduct in any form. This includes, but is not limited to:
*   The use of sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
*   Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments.
*   Public or private harassment of any community member.
*   Trolling, disruptive behavior, or bad-faith interactions.

## Responsibilities & Enforcement
Project maintainers have the authority and responsibility to moderate community interactions. This includes the right to remove or reject comments, commits, code, or issues that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Maintainers who fail to uphold these standards may be removed from the project team.

## Reporting
If you witness or experience behavior that violates these standards, please report it by opening an issue or contacting a project maintainer directly. We are committed to addressing all reports fairly and maintaining the confidentiality of the reporter.

---
*This Code of Conduct is inspired by the spirit of open-source community standards.*
